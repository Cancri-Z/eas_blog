WHAT YOUR DASHBOARD LIGHTS SAYS

Car warning lights are indicators on your dashboard that alert you to potential issues or problems with your vehicle. Here are some common ones:

1. Check Engine Light: Indicates a problem with the engine or its related systems. It could be anything from a loose gas cap to a serious engine malfunction.

2. Oil Pressure Light: Warns you of low oil pressure, which can lead to engine damage if not addressed promptly.

3. Battery Light: Alerts you to issues with the charging system, such as a failing alternator or a loose battery connection.

4. Brake System Light: Indicates a problem with the brake system, such as low brake fluid or worn brake pads.

5. Coolant Temperature Light: Warns of overheating, which could be caused by low coolant levels or a malfunctioning cooling system.

6. ABS Light: Indicates a problem with the anti-lock braking system, which helps prevent skidding during sudden stops.

7. Airbag Light: Alerts you to a problem with the airbag system, indicating that they may not deploy in the event of a collision.

8 Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) Light: Warns you of low tire pressure in one or more tires, which can affect handling and fuel efficiency.

It's important not to ignore these warning lights, as they can indicate serious issues that could jeopardize your safety or lead to expensive repairs if left unaddressed. If any warning light comes on while driving, it's best to pull over when safe to do so and consult your vehicle's manual for guidance on what to do next.
